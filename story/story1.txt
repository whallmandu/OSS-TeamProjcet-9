However, you realize that continuing as you have been won’t change much, so you decide to make a new choice. What will you do?
[2] Request Rescue [3] Remain on the Island

If you have Distress Signal Lv 4:
You used the knowledge you had gained from working as a rescue worker to prepare numerous distress signals on the island. That night, you activated all the devices you had made. Perhaps as a reward for your 30 days of hard work, you soon heard the sound of helicopter blades and voices calling out for you. Rushing out of your shelter, tears filled your eyes as you greeted the rescue team. By coincidence, one of the rescuers was a colleague you had worked with before. While flying back to the city you lived in, you talked with your colleague about your time on the island.
(Rescue Ending)
If you have Distress Signal Lv 3 or below:
You used your rescue-worker knowledge to set up many distress signals on the island. That night, you activated all the devices you had made. However, no sound came by the time dawn broke. Staying calm, you decided to look for another option.
(Back to Choices)

If you have Shelter Lv 4:
 Since the day you were stranded, you used your survival knowledge to secure a living space and adapt to island life. Unlike your previous life filled with tiring work and pressure, you now felt at ease, free from others’ judgment. You realized you didn’t necessarily need to escape. You sat in front of your shelter, watching the sun set over the horizon, holding onto the belief that rescue would eventually come.
(Successful Deserted Ending)
If you have Shelter Lv 3 or below:
Since being stranded, you used your survival knowledge to build a shelter and adapt to island life. Although your shelter wasn’t ideal and you felt uneasy, there was nothing more you could do. You resolved to stay on the island, living each day with the hope that rescue would someday arrive.
(Forced Deserted Ending)
